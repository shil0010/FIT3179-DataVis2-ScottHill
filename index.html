Changes in Agricultural Production in Australia 2000 - 2022

This document displays data from 2000 to 2024 in relation to key agricultural products, namely livestock and crop yields.

It will showcase how Australia's production of these various agriculture products has changed over the years, focusing particularly on the products which have seen the most growth, both positive and negative.

Total Agricultural Production for Australia 2000 - 2022

Of all the livestock raised in Australia Cattle (raised for beef) are BY FAR the most numerous, outnumbering all others by a factor of almost 10!

Although total quantities have shifted downwards over the years in most cases (Beef Cattle are down 9% from 2000), the percentage contributions of each type of livestock has remained almost constant.


Unlike livestock however, crop production has seen a notable INCREASE in recent years, with most crops seeing significant gains in their total yield (tonnes)

Which Product has seen the most growth in recent years

Out of all the agricultural products shown, the Barley crop has exceeded all others in growth, having increased its total yield by roughly 186% since 2000! The second highest growth from any product is a 55% increase in the yield of Oats.

Which Product has seen the most "negative" growth since 2000?

Unfortunately not all of Australia's products have done as well as Barley, the number of cattle raised for dairy and beef in Australia have fallen by approximately 32% and 9% respectively since 2000. As an interesting side-note, 60% of all dairy cattle are located in Victoria despite it having one of the lowest land sizes.

Key Takeaways

Almost all crops have seen a significant increase in production in recent years, unlike livestock which have mostly seen declines in their numbers.

Cattle (Beef) and Barley are both produced / raised in far greater quantities than any other livestock or crop.

Percentage contributions of each type of livestock have remained largely unchanged for the past 20 years.
